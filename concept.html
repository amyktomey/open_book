Meet the Creator, Liz Tomey


“Open Book Catering” (Feed Your Imagination)
This has been a concept in my mind for some time. As a child, I would read anything I could get my hands on. Adventure, mystery, romance… the setting didn’t matter as long as the plot was good. I would get as many books as was allowed (19 a week) and couldn’t wait till Library Friday when I could find a new world to explore.

My grandmother also had a full bookcase and I would hide under the covers pretending to sleep, while reading her books. As I grew older, acting was another outlet for my creativity. I would lose myself in books and characters, as an escape from whatever troubles I had at the time.

Books will forever be a part of my life. Eeveryone needs a little imagination to thrive. These experiences have impacted me in a positive way and I want to share this with everyone. I will give more than just good food. My customers will receive an experience that they will remember forever. The goal is to feed body and soul, providing an escape from the real world for a little while. This is why imagination is so important. It helps get you through the long monotonous days and give joy when everything seems hopeless. It’s what feeds the soul.


Location, location, location!


Louisville is the perfect place to start my catering business. People come from out of town looking for new and exciting adventures. There is always some event taking place. From the Jane Austen festival and Comicon to the Renaissance Faire, there is never a dull moment.

The Kentucky Derby season is also a large attraction and is a busy time for caterers. We (KY) also host a variety of theaters, theme parks, and experiences such as the Bourbon Trail and Light up Louisville. All of this combined makes my location the ideal setting for my target demographic.

For me, catering will start as a mobile business. I will be cooking in test kitchens. I plan on doing events mainly in the homes of my clients, but places like The Brown and The Palace will hold events for parties and groups larger than 20. Events in homes will be limited to 20 guests or less depending on available space. This will give a more personal experience for the enjoyment of my client’s guests.
